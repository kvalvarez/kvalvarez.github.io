Brazoria County Diaper Bank

Brazoria County Diaper Bank is a nonprofit organization dedicated to serving low-income families by providing essential diaper assistance for both babies and elderly individuals in need. We understand that diapers are a basic necessity, yet many families struggle to afford them. Through community support and donations, we strive to ensure that no child or senior has to go without essentials. Our mission is to bring relief, dignity, and support to families, allowing them to focus on building a better future without the stress of diaper insecurity.

Our Mission and Impact

Through strong community partnerships and generous donations, we are able to support families by distributing diapers at no cost. We collaborate with local agencies, churches, and organizations to reach as many individuals as possible, ensuring that assistance is readily available. Our volunteers work tirelessly to collect, organize, and distribute diapers efficiently, making a direct and meaningful impact.

Awareness

1 in 3 U.S families struggle to afford diapers

Many families choose between buying diapers and paying for essentials like rent or food.

Diapers cannot be purchased with government assistance

Programs like SNAP (foodstamps) and WIC do not cover diapers, leaving families with limited options.

Babies need up to 12 diapers per day

Costing around $80-$120 per month. For low-income families, this expense is overwhelming.

Lack of clean diapers can lead to health risks

Babies left in soiled diapers are at higher risk for infections and rashes.
